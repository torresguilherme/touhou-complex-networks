Anyone know how to patch?

(resurrected from Archive 6)

After watching several of our community members make patched games (such as Lucky Charat and RegalStar) I'm interested to get in the mix!

But... after many frustrating Google searches, I got nothing...

Don't worry about the actual translations themselves, I just want to be able to make the files responsible to make this happen.

So, any one willing to help me out? â¥ââ¦ 00:59, 1 April 2011 (UTC)

First off, if you ask a generic question like "how do I create patch", chances are you're probably pretty far away from creating one.
Applying external modifications to a compiled application is just one area of "hacking", and thus requires a comprehensive knowledge on how the program in question, and programs in general, operate. And as every program, and therefore every patch, is different, there's also no "surefire way" to create a patch.
This also explains why your Google searches failed. Nobody can help you if you just ask a generic question like this. See, even I might have misunderstood your question, and you want something entirely different.
I can only suggest you to learn a compiled programming language, ideally C, from start to finish. (Watch out that you learn _only_ C, not C++, at first. All that object-oriented extra stuff may be used by some games, but is not guaranteed to help you much in that regard). Depending of what you want to patch, you may need to learn the basics of assembly language as well.
And after practicing a bit, the basic workflow of "creating a patch" should come to you naturally. If you still need help with specifics then, some hackers will certainly be willing to help you.
If you want to see how such a patch may look like, I recommend taking a look at the source code and technical documentation of the Touhou Vorbis Compressor. I documented all the aspects involved in designing and creating a patch like this. It's a fairly simple patch when compared to other, more complicated patches. If you really want to be able create a patch, you should have no problem understanding this documentation. --NamelessLegacy 11:24, 22 May 2011 (UTC)

Thanks!

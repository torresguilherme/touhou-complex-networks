To all lyric editors: here is a lyric parser that should somewhat make it easier to create articles based on the template. The current design requires you to group your lyrics into stanzas, where single-line space represent lines that are part of the stanza, and a double-line space represents a new stanza. Make sure that the double-line space contains no characters. For example 

line1 (start of stanza 1)
line2
line3 (end of stanza 1)

line4 (start of stanza 2, notice the empty line in between)
line5
line6
...

I assume you will be copy pasting text from an external text editor. Problems occur if you try to manually hit enter on the webpage. I'm not sure why it happens.

Most of it is pretty straightforward. For the album/cover, you would put the album title on the corresponding box, and if that album has a cover, put the name of the image on the right. Similarly for the original title/source, the original title goes on the left and the corresponding source goes on the right.

If the design does not suit your translating style, feel free to offer suggestions on my talk page. --Tsukihime 18:11, 25 March 2011 (UTC)

How to deal with songs used in multiple albums

Previously I had wanted to subpage lyrics cause it makes sense to associate the lyrics for a song with the album that it appears in. But then some circles re-use their songs in multiple albums. Problem becomes "how do we know if a track is re-used?" which I suppose one could simply run a search before writing the lyrics, but that means everytime a new album comes out you'll have to do the check...which is quite tedious. --Tsukihime 13:37, 30 March 2011 (UTC)

One easy way to know if a lyrics page already exists for a song is to simply make the title into a link in the track list, and see wether it's a red link or not. It also means no searching is required either, but it does mean that all lyrics pages need to be named in the proper format (Lyrics: name). If we subpaged all the lyrics it wouldn't be that easy though. Mizutori 13:58, 30 March 2011 (UTC)
BuraQ suggested on IRC that all songs with duplicate names would be placed in the same page in different sections clearly identified. That way, we don't have to deal with searching whether a song is re-used or not, and it would make things simpler --Tsukihime 14:45, 30 March 2011 (UTC)
Okies, I'm good with that too. Mizutori 14:56, 30 March 2011 (UTC)

Standards have been written - Lyrics Guidelines --Tsukihime 15:53, 30 March 2011 (UTC)

A new standard is now being adopted. In case of collisions of titles between different songs each song should be but in a sepparate page with name in the following standard Lyrics:_Song_Title_(Group_Name) as shown here and here, while the old page (one without the group name) should be changed to a disambiguation page as shown here. --BuraQ 17:04, 14 April 2011 (UTC)

General restructuring

Over the past six months or so, I've been redoing a lot of the way content is presented on the Wiki. Every page related to Undefined Fantastic Object, Highly Responsive to Prayers, Story of Eastern Wonderland, and Phantasmagoria of Dim.Dream has been "completed" according to what I was doing, but I'm going to need some help, at least with a few simple tasks. For the time being, there are three things that are relatively simple:

  1. Removing the horizontal lines in between the article body and the navbox
  2. Removing "return to X page" at the top/bottom of pages (this was brought up a while ago, but even with the subpage standard we're using now, there's still a few pages left)
  3. Adding style="border-collapse: collapse;" to every table to get an actual 1-pixel width border (for translation articles and the like)

Darkslime 17:30, 7 April 2011 (UTC)

Also, remember to indicate that a page has been revised by striking out the corresponding entry at the portal. Check which ones have been completed to speed up the process!

The game includes 20 MIDI tracks composed by ZUN for the Roland SC-88Pro module. High quality recordings of these MIDI files made with this module can be found here.

Three unused tracks from the prototype versions of Shuusou Gyoku were posted on ZUN's site, as part of ZUN's Strange Works.

This is the second theme of the final boss, Vivit.

To tell the truth, I wanted to make this mostly a percussive song, but I haven't listened to a lot of songs like that, so it didn't come out as well as I wanted, and I eventually added a melody.

哀しげ～希望～勇敢～再び哀しげ．．．
という曲に仕上げるのに苦労しました。

一分半程の間に目まぐるしく曲調が変化するので、聴く方も大変かも。
イントロは、エロゲーライクに（違）
メインメロディは元気が出るように（謎）

The ending theme.

Sadness, hope, courage, and then, again, sadness...
...Is how it came out, but it was a lot of work.

There's so much going on with so many styles in just a minute and a half that it may be hard to keep track of.
The intro is like something from an eroge (wrong!)
The main melody helps lift up your spirits (somehow)
The main melody is like the cavalry coming in (wrong again)
And the end is based on the ticking of a clock (really)
I worked so much on it, but it's so short~ (^^;

 夜の鳩山を飛ぶ
Yoru no hatoyama wo tobu 		Fly Above Hatoyama at Night

Notes:

This song is from the prototype version of the game. It was shared by ZUN in 2004, then extended and included on the CD included with Perfect Memento in Strict Sense.

th98_00 from [|ZUN's old blog]

ハードディスクを眺めていたら懐かしいデータを発掘した。
この曲に覚えがある方がもう殆ど存在しないと思うので公開してみたり。

これは、大学時代に学園祭中に作曲したものです。
後輩のゲーム（ＳＴＧ）に曲が無かった為、テキストエディタしかない場所で即興で創ったものです。
驚くことに、メロディは想像しながらテキストエディタだけで創った曲なんですよね。15分位で……
今では考えられないほどハイテンションな人でした。（いや今も躁だってば）

When going through my hard drive, I managed to find some old data. I think that nearly everyone forgot about this song by now, so I'm sharing it with the public.

This was composed during a college festival. My junior classmates had no music for their shooting game, so I improvised something in a text editor.

Unusually, I thought up the melody and wrote it out using nothing but the text editor. It took only 15 minutes, too.

I can't imagine doing something like that today. I'm more of a "high-tension" person nowadays. (That's nothing new.)

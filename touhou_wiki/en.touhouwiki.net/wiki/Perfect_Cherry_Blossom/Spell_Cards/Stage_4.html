Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ 1.0 1.1 Dinning: striking with confusing or clanging sounds; to stun with loud and continued noise.
  2. ↑ 2.0 2.1 Poltergeist: a "noisy" variety of ghost that can manifest themselves visibly, audibly and solidly to the material world. The Prismriver Sisters are poltergeists.
  3. ↑ 3.0 3.1 Guarneri del Gesù: the working pseudonym of Giuseppe Guarneri, who is regarded as one of the finest violin makers in history. The quality of his instruments are generally considered second only to those of Antonio Stradivari.
  4. ↑ 4.0 4.1 弦奏 (Gensou) (lit. 'string performance'): homophone of 幻想 (Gensou) ('fantasy').
  5. ↑ 5.0 5.1 Stradivarius: a term used to refer to the instruments created by Antonio Stradivari. Stradivari's instruments are regarded as amongst the finest stringed instruments ever created, are highly prized, and still played by professionals today.
  6. ↑ 6.0 6.1 "Hino" most likely refers to Terumasa Hino, who is arguably Asia's most renowned jazz trumpeter.
  7. ↑ 7.0 7.1 "Clifford" most likely refers to Clifford Brown, who was considered to be one of the most remarkable trumpeters of the 1950s, and was one of the largest influences on jazz.
  8. ↑ 8.0 8.1 8.2 冥 (Mei) ('dark/dim/gloomy'): possibly a pun on 名 (Mei) as in 名器 (Meiki) ('famous instrument')?
  9. ↑ 9.0 9.1 "Fazioli": a piano manufacturer that builds very high-quality pianos.
  10. ↑ 10.0 10.1 冥奏 (Gensou) (lit. 'dim/gloomy performance'): homophone of 瞑想 (Meisou) ('meditation')?
  11. ↑ 11.0 11.1 "Bösendorfer": the oldest piano manufacturer still producing its own instruments today and has produced some of the finest instruments in the world. Made famous for building the only pianos that could withstand Franz Liszt's tremendously powerful playing during his time.
  12. ↑ 12.0 12.1 "Funeral Concert" is an attempt at capturing a pun: 合葬 (Gathering Funeral) and 合奏 (Concert) are both pronounced the same way in Japanese. "Concerto": a piece of music where two unequal groups of instruments play, the smaller group (usually a soloist) taking the main melodic role, and the larger group acting as accompaniment.
  13. ↑ 13.0 13.1 "Stygian Riverside": in Greek mythology, Styx is the name of the river which formed the boundary between Earth and the underworld, Hades.
  14. ↑ 14.0 14.1 14.2 14.3 Concerto grosso: A concerto subtype, where the smaller section is not a mere soloist, but a group of performers. "Ghostly Wheel": an attempt to translate "霊車", which can also translate to "Funeral Car".
  15. ↑ revised (改) and wonderous (怪) are both pronounced "kai".

Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ Northeastern Japan is home to many shrines with similar names such as Niwatari Jinja, O-niwatari Jinja, Niwatori Jinja, Miwatari Jinja, etc., with a wide variety of kanji readings for each. A number of these shrines have connections to chickens ("niwatori" in Japanese), enshrine the god Ame-no-Mikumari-no-Kami (天水分神, lit. "heavenly water-sharing god"), and/or hold rituals where water is splashed on images of chickens in order to cure throat illnesses.
  2. ↑ All of Kutaka's cards on Hard and Lunatic change the kanji 練 (ren) in 試練 (shiren, "ordeal") into 煉 (ren), from 煉獄 (rengoku, "purgatory").
  3. ↑ 見渡神社 (Miwatashi Jinja, lit. "surveying shrine"): the name of several shrines in northeastern Japan, sometimes referred to as Niwatari Jinja. See the footnote above.
  4. ↑ 鬼渡神社 (Oniwatari Jinja, lit. "oni's passage shrine"): the name of several shrines in northeastern Japan, related to other shrines with similar names and to chickens ("niwatori"). See the footnotes above.
  5. ↑ Due to a bug, this card is sometimes incorrectly displayed in place of Stage 2's final card in the trial version's Player Data section.
  6. ↑ 獄級 (Gokukyuu, lit. "Hell-Level") is a pun on the word 極級 (Gokukyuu, lit. "Ultimate")

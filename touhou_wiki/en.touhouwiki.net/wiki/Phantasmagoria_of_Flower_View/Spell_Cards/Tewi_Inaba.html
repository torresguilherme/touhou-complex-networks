Spell Card Charge
「二兎追」 ("Nitotsui")
"Chasing Two Rabbits"
Owner
Tewi Inaba
Editor's Comment
Two trails of flame appear to either side of Tewi and each trail moves to the opposite side, crossing each other to the front of her.
Spell Card EX
「兎玉」 ("Togyoku")
"Rabbit Orb"
Owner
Tewi Inaba
Editor's Comment
Much like Reimu's EX. A ball appears at the top of the opponent's screen, and moves downwards, floating towards and bouncing off the sides of the screen in the process. The ball maintains a constant horizontal velocity, and has its "gravity" oriented toward one of the sides of the screen, so it only bounces along one side.
Spell Card Lv2
兎符「開運大紋」 (Tofu "Kaiun Daimon")
Rabbit Sign "Great Fortune Crest"
Owner
Tewi Inaba
Editor's Comment
A cluster of bullets appear in the center of the opponent's screen, and expand into several overlapping rings of bullets. These rings of bullets also revolve in opposing directions. Every other bullet in each ring is removable, and because they are densely packed when they first appear, one well placed explosion from a fairy or ghost bullet is enough to remove all of them if it is done quickly enough.
Spell Card Lv3
兎符「開運大紋」 (Tofu "Kaiun Daimon")
Rabbit Sign "Great Fortune Crest"
Owner
Tewi Inaba
Editor's Comment
Same as her Lv2, except that there are more bullets and all the bullets are now non-removable.
Spell Card Boss
兎符「因幡の素兎」 (Tofu "Inaba no Shirousagi")
Rabbit Sign "Bare White Rabbit of Inaba"
Owner
Tewi Inaba
Editor's Comment
Boss summon. It is a reference to the "bare rabbit of Inaba" or "naked rabbit of Inaba" from the episode of the Inaba and Oomunachi (オオナムチ, 大国主). 素兎 is read "Shiro-usagi." "Shiro" means white, but was written with 素, which is not usually read that way and means "bare," indicating a double meaning that the white is "bareness." Thus, the double meaning of "The White Rabbit of Inaba" and the "The Bare/Naked Rabbit of Inaba."

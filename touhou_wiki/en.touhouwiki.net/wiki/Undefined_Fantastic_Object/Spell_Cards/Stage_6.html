Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ 1.0 1.1 1.2 1.3 The Amitabha Buddha is said to appear riding on purple clouds to welcome boddhisatvas into the Pure Land. In the third chapter of the Shigisan Chronicle (信貴山縁起, Shigisan Engi), the sister of the monk Myouren is guided to him by a bank of purple clouds.
  2. ↑ This is most likely a reference to Shinki's second-to-last attack.
  3. ↑ 3.0 3.1 Amagimi (尼公), also pronounced "Nikou," is a respectful term for a Buddhist nun. The third chapter of the Shigisan Chronicle (信貴山縁起, Shigisan Engi), the Amagimi-no-maki, depicts the monk Myouren's sister as she searches for his whereabouts. "Air Scroll" (エア巻物, ea-makimono) is likely a pun on "picture scroll" (絵巻物, e-makimono).

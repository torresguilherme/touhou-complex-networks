Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ Seiga Kaku's spell card titles have kana representations of Chinese, using the ancient pronunciations Japan uses for Chinese vocabulary.
    養小鬼: A sorcery which involves taming dead spirits using dead foetus corpses to use as tools.
  2. ↑ 孤魂野鬼: Abandoned wild ghosts. A metaphor that represents struggling people with nothing to rely on.
  3. ↑ 走火入魔: A state of frenzy often associated with the mismanagement of the "vital forces".
  4. ↑ Tongji (童乩): A ritual where a person gets possessed by a divine spirit. Under this state of trance the medium can withstand great pain, like getting impaled through the cheek. It is rendered タンキー (Tankii) in Japanese because Japan took the word from the Minnan dialect.
  5. ↑ Tongling (通靈): Spirit linking.
  6. ↑ The name (タオ胎動 = tao taidou) is a pun on the video game Tao Taidou (タオ体動).

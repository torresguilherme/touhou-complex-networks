Midboss Spell Cards[edit]

Spell Card 107
アンノウン「軌道不明の鬼火」 (Announ "Kinou Fumei no Onibi")
Unknown "Will-o'-wisps in Unknown Orbit"
Owner
Nue Houjuu
Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
Confused stars sway back and forth drunkenly.
Spell Card 108
アンノウン「姿態不明の空魚」 (Announ "Shitai Fumei no Kuugyo")
Unknown "Skyfish with Unknown Shape" [1]
Owner
Nue Houjuu
Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
An optical phenomenom where fast objects appear elongated. Lots of people think that they're aliens.
Spell Card 109
アンノウン「原理不明の妖怪玉」 (Announ "Genri Fumei no Youkai-gyoku")
Unknown "Youkai Orb of Unknown Mechanics"
Owner
Nue Houjuu
Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
A mysterious orb that follows you around.
Draw near to the source of the undefined.

Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
Dogs, Birds, Frogs, everyone is here for a good party.
Stay out of the middle when the frogs explode, and keep moving vertically to trick the dogs.

Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
One can only imagine the strangeness of a youkai school.
This is a simulation for when she teaches the youkai to stand up against Miko.
Spell Card 116
七番勝負「野生の離島」 (Nanaban Shoubu "Yasei no Ritou")
Seventh Duel "Wild Deserted Island"
Owner
Mamizou Futatsuiwa
Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
The island that isn't deserted at all.
The dogs chase the birds, the birds chase the shrine maidens.
Spell Card 117
変化「まぬけ巫女の偽調伏」 (Henge "Manuke Miko no Nise-choufuku")
Transformation "Pseudo-Exorcism of the Stupid Shrine Maiden" [3]
Owner
Mamizou Futatsuiwa
Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
Time to be a shrine maiden! Exterminating humans with exploding frogs is what shrine maidens should do.
Spell Card 118
「マミゾウ化弾幕十変化」 ("Mamizou-ka Danmaku Juuhenge")
"Mamizou Danmaku in Ten Transformations"
Owner
Mamizou Futatsuiwa
Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
Do your best to avoid birds and dogs while being trapped by enroaching humans.
Spell Card 119
狢符「満月のポンポコリン」 (Mujina-fu "Mangetsu no Ponpokorin")
Mujina Sign "Full Moon Pompokolin" [4] [5]
Owner
Mamizou Futatsuiwa
Extra — Extra
Editor's Comment
The frogs dance the full moon dance.
Ponpoko!

Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ Skyfish is another name for the flying rods that are often captured on tape but never in real life
  2. ↑ The famous caricature scroll set Choujuu-jinbutsu-giga
  3. ↑ まぬけ(Stupid) is a reference to Taito's game, KiKi KaiKai, which the Touhou Project as a whole is inspired by. The stage 7 boss is a tanuki named Manuke (魔奴化). He was disguised as the shrine maiden Sayo-chan, the main character of that series.
  4. ↑ Mujina: The people of Sado called tanuki "mujina".
  5. ↑ "Ponpoko" is the onomatopoeia for the sound of a tanuki drum. May also be related to Odoru Pompokolin.

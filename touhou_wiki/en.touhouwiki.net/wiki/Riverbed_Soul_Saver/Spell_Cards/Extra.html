Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ Super-rotation, in which a planet's atmosphere can do a whole revolution in the span of four days, occurs on the planet Venus, also known as Ame no Kagaseo in Japanese mythology.
  2. ↑ Megiddo is a tell in northern Israel, mentioned in the Book of Revelations as the site of a military gathering during the Apocalypse. Kimi ga Yobu, Megido no Oka de (君が呼ぶメギドの丘で) is also the name of an RPG video game, titled "Voices from Har Megiddo" for its English release.
  3. ↑ A pun on "Uchide no Kozuchi" and "Nozuchi", a snake youkai.
  4. ↑ 流星光底 (ryuusei koutei) is a four-character idiom referring to the shimmering, comet-like trail (ostensibly) left by a sword swing.
  5. ↑ A pun on "extend" and "Excalibur".
  6. ↑ A reference to a legend about Princess Yamatototohimomosohime (倭迹迹日百襲媛命), one of the inspirations for Momohime's character. In the legend, the princess was to be married to the god Oomononushi, but accidentally stabbed herself with chopsticks in fright upon discovering that her husband-to-be was secretly a snake. She was subsequently buried in the Chopstick Tomb kofun.
  7. ↑ Totsuka no Tsurugi, meaning "ten-hand sword", (ie. a sword with length ten times that of its handle) is one of several names for the sword that Susanoo used to kill Orochi. The way the name of this Spell Card is written ("____ Sword" in kanji, then "____ Blade" in katakana) may also be a reference to Sakuya Izayoi's "Silver Blade" shot from Double Dealing Character.
  8. ↑ A pun on the pronunciation of "Midgardsormr" ("Midgard Serpent"), which is another name for the Norse world-snake Jörmungandr. "Form" may be a reference to the 'form changes' in the Pokemon series; the developers of Riverbed Soul Saver have jokingly referred to the game's power-up system as being inspired by the Mega Evolutions from Pokemon as well.
  9. ↑ This is a pun on the name Yamata-no-Orochi (八岐の大蛇, "Eight-headed serpent") and on Yamatai (邪馬台), the purported kingdom that was ruled over by Queen Himiko.

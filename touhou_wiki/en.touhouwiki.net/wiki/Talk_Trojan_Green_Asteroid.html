Based on the Jupiter Trojans?[edit]

I find that the title is somewhat based on the Jupiter Trojans. As usual, I could be mistaken, but should this be noted? ～ Tony64 (Talk/Con.) 23:21, 15 April 2012 (UTC)

After investigating some blog posts, I find wikipedia:Trojan (astronomy) to be more relevant. This is confirmed by the title of one of the songs, which contains トロヤ群 (Wikipedia page).--としあき 23:38, 15 April 2012 (UTC)
Not enough context as of now. The Jupiter Trojans are the most well-known, and are colloquially called "Trojans", so I wouldn't be surprised if there was a greater reference to them. But, with no current context (only in the titles, which are insufficient), we have to stick with the generic Trojans. If there were some story explaining Trojans, then we can learn more from that. - Kiefmaster99 23:50, 15 April 2012 (UTC)
Also, the use of トロヤ群 is not definitive. "単に「トロヤ群」という場合は通常「木星のトロヤ群」を意味する。" - Kiefmaster99 23:57, 15 April 2012 (UTC)
After reading some TLs, it's definitely referring to generic Trojans (i.p. with respect to Earth and Moon). - Kiefmaster99 14:27, 5 May 2012 (UTC)

Another Stike?[edit]

I've noticed the sudden disappearing of a few Touhou OST uploaders and the tracks of Trojan Green Asteroid on Youtube and wanted to know what's going on exactly, I've been hearing a few rumours about ZUN not wanting to have his album all over the place while it's still selling and a few other rumours that are a bit stranger. Just wanted to know what's going on.

I checked my history for one of the videos I watched and it's true, it was taken off due to a copyright claim from Team Shanghai Alice, and he also posted something on Twitter that seemingly has something to do with it (当然、CDを動画にしてアップするのは二次創作ではないので許可出来ないよん). Of course, he has every right to defend his property, and his decision should be respected. U❊T❊W 09:38, 5 May 2012 (UTC)

Story Mode Cards[edit]

Spell Card Story Mode 1
符の壱「幽夢の胡蝶」 (Fu no Ichi "Yuumu no Kochou")
First Card "Butterfly from a Ghostly Dream"
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Sakuya Stage 5, Patchouli Stage 5, Remilia Stage 5, Yukari Stage 6
Editor's Comment
Yuyuko releases several lines of butterflies in a ring around her, which then slowly drift outward before fading away.
Spell Card Story Mode 2
符の弐「白玉楼の垂れ彼岸」 (Fu no Ni "Hakugyokurou no Tare Higan")
Second Card "Weeping Higan Cherry of Hakugyokurou"
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Sakuya Stage 5, Patchouli Stage 5, Remilia Stage 5, Yukari Stage 6
Editor's Comment
Yuyuko releases a fountain of ghosts, followed by individual ghosts falling from the top of the screen for the remainder of the card.
Spell Card Story Mode 3
符の参「果てしなく昔の死地」 (Fu no San "Hateshinaku Mukashi no Shichi")
Third Card "Distantly Ancient Place of Death"
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Sakuya Stage 5, Patchouli Stage 5, Remilia Stage 5, Yukari Stage 6
Editor's Comment
Yuyuko spawns fountains of ghosts on the ground (either in rapid succession beneath the player, or stretching across the whole floor at once).
Spell Card Story Mode 4
桜花「未練未酌宴」 (Ouka "Mirenmishaku-en")
Cherry Blossom "Feast of Lingering Regrets"
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Sakuya Stage 5, Patchouli Stage 5, Remilia Stage 5, Yukari Stage 6
Editor's Comment
Yuyuko floats in midair with her fan, firing bursts of cherry petals towards the player and intermittently releasing rings of bubble bullets.
Spell Card Story Mode 5
「紫(むらさき)の彼岸は遅れて輝く」 ("Murasaki no Higan wa Okurete Kagayaku")
"The Purple Higan Shines Late" [1]
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Yukari Stage 6
Editor's Comment
Yuyuko floats in the center of the screen, releasing intersecting rings of butterflies. Lasers rotate around her in set intervals, creating pillars of light when they touch the floor.

Player Spell Cards[edit]

Spell Card A-1
死符「ギャストリドリーム」 (Shifu "Gyasutori Doriimu")
Death Sign "Ghastly Dream"
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Player - Initial
Editor's Comment
Releases a ring of intersecting butterflies that expands across the screen.
Spell Card A-2
死蝶「華胥の永眠」 (Shichou "Kasho no Eimin")
Deadly Butterfly "Eternal Sleep in Dreamland" [2]
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Player - Final
Editor's Comment
Releases two rings of intersecting butterflies that expand across the screen.
Spell Card B-2
冥符「黄泉平坂行路」 (Meifu "Yomotsu-Hirasaka Kouro")
Nether Sign "Path to Yomotsu-Hirasaka" [3]
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Player - Initial
Editor's Comment
Sends a shockwave forward along the ground, releasing ghosts that slowly float upward in its wake.
Spell Card B-2
再迷「幻想郷の黄泉還り」 (Saimei "Gensoukyou no Yomigaeri")
Lost Again "Gensokyo's Rise from Yomi" [3]
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Player - Final
Editor's Comment
Creates a large geyser of ghosts at the opponent's feet.
Spell Card C-1
霊符「无寿の夢」 (Reifu "Tenju no Yume")
Spirit Sign "Ageless Dream" [4]
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Player - Initial
Editor's Comment
Fires a small wisp at the opponent. If the hit connects, the opponent is surrounded by wisps, slowly draining their health until the wisps disappear.
Spell Card C-2
寿命「无寿国への約束手形」 (Jumyou "Tenjukoku he no Yakusoku Tegata")
Lifespan "Ticket to the Ageless Land" [4]
Owner
Yuyuko Saigyouji
Player - Final
Editor's Comment
Fires a small wisp at the opponent. If the hit connects, the opponent is surrounded by wisps, slowly draining their health until the wisps disappear.

Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ The kanji 紫 (purple) is also used in Yukari's name. While in this case it's explicitly read as "murasaki" and not "yukari", the name of the card can also be taken as commentary on Yukari's longetivity (as Higan will shine later for her than for others).
  2. ↑ 華胥 (Kasho in Japanese, Huaxu in Chinese) is a Chinese utopia described in the Liezi. The Yellow Emperor was said to have gone to Huaxu in his dreams. Once he woke up, he became enlightened.
  3. ↑ 3.0 3.1 Yomi: the land of the dead in Shinto mythology. Yomotsu-Hirasaka: the slope leading down to Yomi.
  4. ↑ 4.0 4.1 A reference to the "Mandala of the Land of Heavenly Life" (天寿国繍帳, tenjukoku shūchō), commissioned after the death of Prince Shōtoku. "天寿" (heavenly life) may possibly be a misprint of "无寿" (roughly 'non-life'), which is the phrase used in this spellcard.

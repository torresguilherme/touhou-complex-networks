Suika beats Reimu[edit]

Suika

何処に居たのか判らなかったよ。
戦ってる感じがしなくて面白くないわ。

Where were you?
If you don't feel like fighting, it's not fun.

Suika beats Marisa[edit]

Suika

非力だなぁ、人間は。
私なら薬指だけで勝てるよ。

Humans are so helpless.
I could win with only my ring finger.

Suika beats Sakuya[edit]

Suika

あんたのナイフはスカスカね。
今度、究極の密度を見せてあげるよ？

Your knives are full of holes.
Next time, I'll show you what real density is.

Suika beats Alice[edit]

Suika

多勢に無勢。そんな小編隊で、私の夢幻の大群に敵
う筈がないわ。

Strength in numbers. Such a small army couldn't possibly
stand up to my phantom hordes.

Suika beats Patchouli[edit]

Suika

そんな人工の自然。
大自然に敵う筈が無い。大は少を兼ねるよ。

Your artificial nature...
will never be able to stand up to real nature. A large thing will serve for a small one.

Suika beats Youmu[edit]

Suika

物を斬る？何でそんなまどろっこしい事してるの？
山を砂にするまで何回斬ればいいの？

Cutting things? Why are you doing something so slow?
How many times do you need to cut before a mountain becomes sand?

Suika beats Remilia[edit]

Suika

幾ら速く動いたって無駄だって。
まさに釈迦の掌の上よ。

No matter how fast you move, it's useless.
You'll always be on Buddha's palm.[1]

Suika beats Yuyuko[edit]

Suika

無念無想、か。まだまだ雑念が残ってるよ。
そんなんだから成仏出来ないのさ。

Emptiness, huh? You still have worldly thoughts in your head.
You'll never reach nirvana this way.

Suika beats Yukari[edit]

Suika

今日はどうしたの？
いつものキレが、いつも無いじゃない。

What's wrong today?
You're not your usual self.

Suika beats Meiling[edit]

Suika

気を扱える程度じゃ話にならないね
私は大気だって思いのままなのさ

Your chi can't beat my chi.
I AM chi.

Suika beats someone[edit]

Suika

正直言うと……
弱い者いじめかなぁ、とか思っちゃったりして

To be honest...
I feel like I'm picking on a weakling.

Suika

今日はこの位にしてやるよ。
正直、止め時が判らないから。

I'll stop here.
To be honest, I don't actually know when to stop.

Suika

ごめんごめん。
ちょっと飲みすぎたかなぁ？

Sorry, sorry.
I drank too much.

Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ This phrase is a reference to a story from Xiyouji: When Sun Wukong is scolded by Gautama Buddha for making several attempts to resist deity's sanctions, he says proudly to Buddha that he has mastered all the 36 mysterical arts and is able to fly one hundred and eight thousand Li (approximately fifty-four thousand kilometers) in a moment riding on Jindouyun. Hearing this, Buddha makes a bet that Wukong cannot get out from his right palm. Wukong laughs at this bet and flies out on Jindouyun. In a few seconds, he reaches five pillars and assumes that this is the end of the world. He leaves his signature on a pillar, urinates on it, and comes back. Then, Buddha shows one of his finger, where Wukong finds his signature and loses.

Qian & Kun "Violent Land that Mothers All"
乾坤「荒々しくも母なる大地よ」 Qian & Kun "Violent Land that Mothers All"
• 使用者　比那名居天子 • User: Tenshi Hinanawi
• 備考　天界で見た • Notes: Saw it in Heaven
• 参考度　真似出来そうにない • Reference Level: Don't think I can imitate it
地面が激しくでこぼこになるスペルカード。 A Spell Card that violently disrupts the ground.
恐ろしい程のパワーで地面に要石を叩きつけると、その反動か他の地面が持ち上がる。 Tenshi slams a keystone into the ground with fearsome power, and the surrounding ground raises up, presumably in response.
箪笥の引き出しみたいなもんだろう。勢いよく閉めると他の引き出しが飛び出すという。私にも真似出来るのか？　いや無理か。そんなに重くないしな。 It's probably like a chest of drawers; slamming one shut makes the others pop out. Can I imitate this? Nah, probably not. I ain't that heavy, after all.
Spirit Thought "Stone that Calms the Lands"
霊想「大地を鎮める石」 Spirit Thought "Stone that Calms the Lands"
• 使用者　比那名居天子 • User: Tenshi Hinanawi
• 備考　漬け物石 • Notes: Pickling weights
• 参考度　陰陽玉とどっこいどっこい • Reference Level: 'Bout the same as the yin-yang orbs
ボコボコになった大地を鎮めるように光が降り注ぎ、それが要石となる。 Light pours down on the wrecked earth, as if to quell it, and it turns into keystones.
私も天子みたいに要石に乗りたいのだが、何故か乗れない。 I wanna ride a keystone like Tenshi, but for some reason I can't stay on the things.
觔斗雲 (きんとんうん) みたいなもんか。 Is it a Flying Nimbus kinda deal? [1]
Heaven & Earth "Land that Oversees the Distant World Below"
天地「世界を見下ろす遥かなる大地よ」 Heaven & Earth "Land that Oversees the Distant World Below"
• 使用者　比那名居天子 • User: Tenshi Hinanawi
• 備考　工事現場 • Notes: Construction site
• 参考度　なるわけがない • Reference Level: Like heck I could
地面が際限なく盛り上がっていくスペルカード。 A Spell Card where the ground rises upward without limit.
天子が剣で地面を刺すとそこが盛り上がり、最終的に宇宙に飛び出すまで盛り上がり続ける。 When Tenshi stabs her sword into a patch of ground, it rises up, and it'll just keep rising until it eventually shoots into space.
高いところが苦手な人間に大ダメージを与えるらしい。 Deals heavy damage to humans who're afraid of heights, apparently.
こいつのスペルカードは基本、弾幕ではないな。 Her Spell Cards basically ain't danmaku.
"Sky of Scarlet Perception of All Humankind"
「全人類の緋想天」 "Sky of Scarlet Perception of All Humankind"
• 使用者　比那名居天子 • User: Tenshi Hinanawi
• 備考　似たタイプの技を持っている • Notes: I've got some similar moves myself
• 参考度　★★★ • Reference Level: ★★★
叩き付けるような気の極太レーザー。 A massive spirit laser that practically slams into ya.
私のマスタースパークと同じようなスペルカードに見えるが、これはレーザーに見えるが実はレーザーではなく、超高速、超高密度の気弾の集まりである。 It looks like the same kinda spell as my Master Spark, but this one actually ain't a laser. It's a super-fast an' super-dense mass of concentrated spirit bullets.
つまり、当たると超痛い。 That is to say, it hurts like hell.
Spirit Sign "State of Freedom from Worldly Thoughts"
気符「無念無想の境地」 Spirit Sign "State of Freedom from Worldly Thoughts"
• 使用者　比那名居天子 • User: Tenshi Hinanawi
• 備考　天界で見た、ドーピングタイプ • Notes: Saw it in Heaven, doping type
• マゾ度　★★★★★ • Masochist Level: ★★★★★
一切痛さを感じなくなるという、非常に特殊なスペルカード。 An extremely unique Spell Card where she becomes unable to feel any pain.
特殊だが便利な気もする。冬場の冷え込む時とか、長時間労働とか。 It's unusual, but seems kinda convenient too. Like for when it gets cold in winter, or when you've gotta work long hours, or such.
Earthquake "Sword of Pleasure After Hardship"
地震「先憂後楽の剣」 Earthquake "Sword of Pleasure After Hardship"
• 使用者　比那名居天子 • User: Tenshi Hinanawi
• 備考　防災の準備が必要、迷惑 • Notes: Gotta prep for disasters in advance, annoying
• 迷惑度　★★★★★★ • Annoyance Level: ★★★★★★
力一杯大地を刺激し、忘れた頃に地震が起こる迷惑なスペルカード。 A real pesky Spell Card where she stimulates the earth with all her might, an' then an earthquake happens once just enough time has passed for you to forget about it.
天災は忘れた頃にやってくるとはよく言った物で、こいつのスペルカードも忘れた頃にやってくる。 People say that natural disasters strike just when you've forgotten about 'em, and her Spell Cards strike the same way. [2]
地震は起きてしまったら避けようがないので、取り敢えず地面から離れるのがいいだろう。 There's no way to dodge an earthquake once it happens, so you oughta just get off the ground in advance.


Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ The Flying Nimbus from Dragon Ball is a flying cloud that Son Goku travels on. People with impure hearts cannot ride it. It's based on clouds from Journey to the West, though those don't have the property Marisa refers to here.
  2. ↑ Reference to a famous axiom attributed to Meiji-era physicist Torahiko Terada — "the natural disasters come when we have forgotten them" (天災は忘れた頃にやって来る, Tensai wa wasureta koro ni yattekuru).

東　方　大　改　訂

Welcome to 東方大改訂 (Touhou Daikaitei, "Great Revisioning of the East"), the great literature revisal project for Touhou Wiki!

Collating materials and resources

Introduction[edit]

The majority of information on Touhou Wiki revolves around the translation of the various written pieces available, either ZUN and Co.'s various written works, game manuals and scripts, interviews, etc. Specifically, the reliability of the entire wiki depends on the reliable transcription and translation of said documentation. Over the years we've had many edits of very varying quality, to both transcribed text and its translation. The errors between each stage in the process can compound, allowing disinformation to cloud the wiki's accuracy. The process of this project aims to collate all of the available materials, including those which we're missing to date, retranscribing all of the works, and then retranslating. Thus we can ensure we have as little loss of information in the source materials we base our wiki work on.

The process[edit]

The process will take place in a few steps. First off, note that this project will move forward mostly in chronological order. It was decided to do this for a couple reasons: one, so we can get our act together and see how this whole thing works before getting to the more popular articles, and two, so that we maintain translation consistency and are able to gain a better grasp on ZUN's written dialogue, as well as build up a sort of "vocabulary" of sorts for Touhou-specific terminology. Another reason is that short story/manga releases are serialized, and since we're breaking everything up into groups (see below) it works out well to do single chapters at a time.

The first step will be transcription (where needed), or else a copy of whatever source material to translate (readme files, etc.). We don't have to have every single thing ever transcribed right away, and transcribers can generally go in any order they want! However, they will want to stay roughly in chronological order because that is how the translation will be happening.

We will decide on a group of materials to translate at once, say all of TH01 and TH02's resources. Once we gather the relevant files and/or transcribe anything needed, we will "activate" the group for translation. While active, someone will put up a Google Document for each of the materials. This way, we can have multiple people collaborating on the translations, which solves our past problems of having a one-person translation machine.

Each material will be required to have at least two people having worked on the translation. Once the entire thing is translated, the material will be marked for revision. A separate party will then go in, check the translation, and if everything's good, we declare it complete and place it on the wiki itself.

All progress would be kept track of on this page, of course. As you may have noticed, if we go in chronological order, there will not be too much to transcribe at the beginning. Apart from the dialogue from the early PC-98 games, which I believe needs to be transcribed by hand, anyone doing transcribing is generally encouraged to transcribe what they want. When doing transcriptions, please keep your copy on-hand and update this page with your progress, so that if you fall off the face of the earth, we can pick up where you left off.

I (Darkslime) propose that our first "group" be the materials from TH01 and TH02 - this means the readme for each, the extra/bonus text file for each, the endings for each, and the music room and dialogue for TH02. Once these materials are all gathered and transcribed, others can begin translation.

Story Mode Cards[edit]

Spell Card Story Mode 1
光弾「ドップラーエフェクト」 (Koudan "Doppuraa Efekuto")
Photon "Doppler Effect"
Owner
Yukari Yakumo
Youmu Stage 4, Remilia Stage 8, Reisen Stage 3, Iku Stage 5
Editor's Comment
Fires blue bullets that don't stop, as well as red bullets that stop for a short time and then move again on Normal or harder.
Spell Card Story Mode 2
捌器「全てを二つに別ける物」 (Hakki "Subete wo Futatsu ni Wakeru-mono")
Splitter "Thing That Splits All Into Two"
Owner
Yukari Yakumo
Youmu Stage 4, Remilia Stage 8, Reisen Stage 3, Iku Stage 5
Editor's Comment
Sinks into a gap, then reappears as a bullet-emitting sawblade that either leaps up from the ground or moves horizontally through the air.
Spell Card Story Mode 3
幻巣「飛光虫ネスト」 (Gensou "Hikouchuu Nesuto")
Fantasy Nest "Flying Noctilucae's Nest" [1]
Owner
Yukari Yakumo
Youmu Stage 4, Remilia Stage 8, Reisen Stage 3, Iku Stage 5
Editor's Comment
Also a player spellcard. Fires a lengthy barrage of lasers horizontally in the player's direction.
Spell Card Story Mode 4
空餌「狂躁高速飛行物体」 (Kuuji "Kyousou Kousoku Hikoubuttai")
Aerial Bait "Hyperactive High Speed Flying Object"
Owner
Yukari Yakumo
Remilia Stage 8, Reisen Stage 3, Iku Stage 5
Editor's Comment
Alternates between summoning targets that lasers converge on, and sweeping the screen with horizontal lasers.
Spell Card Story Mode 5
「八雲の巣」 ("Yakumo no Su")
"Yakumo's Nest" [2]
Owner
Yukari Yakumo
Remilia Stage 8
Editor's Comment
Fires paralyzing lasers, which then emit a web of paralyzing laser bullets.

Skill Cards[edit]



Skill Card 1
開けて悔しき玉手箱 (Akete Kuyashiki Tamatebako")
Urashima's Box, Filled With Lament [3]
Cost
1 orb
Comment
上空に隙間を開き、漂流物を落下
相手に直撃させて攻撃する
Translation
Opens a gap in the sky and drops debris from it directly onto the opponent.

{{{keycommand}}}


Skill Card 2
禅寺に潜む妖蝶 (Zen-ji ni Hisomu Youchou)
Bewitching Butterfly Lurking in the Zen Temple
Cost
1 orb
Comment
傘を回して攻撃する打撃技
無敵時間は無いが判定は強力
Translation
A melee move that attacks by whirling her umbrella. Has no invincibility period, but its hitbox is bulky.
Move Command
{{{keycommand}}}


Skill Card 3
枕石漱流
漱石枕流 (Chinseki Souryuu)
Rest on Stone, Rinse in Rivers [4]
Rinse with Stone, Rest in Rivers [5]
Cost
1 orb
Comment
弱で飛び道具を吸い込む穴を開く
吸い込んだ物はカウントされ
強で出す「漱石枕流」によって
放出することが出来る
Translation
'Rest on Stone' (B) opens up a hole that sucks in projectiles. The sucked-in objects are stored, and can be shot out using 'Rinse with Stone' (C).
Move Command
{{{keycommand}}}


Skill Card 4
幻想狂想穴 (Gensou Kyousouketsu)
Fantasy Maniacal Rift
Cost
1 orb
Comment
隙間の中に退避して移動しつつ
出口から攻撃しつつ登場する
出すボタンとキー入力で攻撃内容
出現地点を制御できる
Translation
Retreats into a gap while moving, then appears from the exit while attacking. The attack button used controls the form of the attack, and the arrow keys control where you emerge.
Move Command
{{{keycommand}}}


Skill Card 5
至る処に青山あり (Itaru Koro ni Seizan Ari)
Home is Where One Lays to Rest [6]
Cost
1 orb
Comment
隙間から漂流物を突き出して攻撃
扇状に近い距離を広くカバーし
中距離の牽制技になる
Translation
An attack that thrusts debris out from a gap, covering a wide, roughly fan-shaped area. Good as a mid-range deterrent.
Move Command
{{{keycommand}}}


Skill Card 6
幻想卍傘 (Gensou Bangasa)
Fantasy Manji-Parasol
Cost
1 orb
Comment
傘の上に妖力を卍状に収束し
それを回転させ飛ばす飛び道具
判定が大きく敵に食らい付き拘束
ホールドで射出を遅らせられるが
この状態では攻撃力が無い
Translation
Concentrates spirit aura into a manji shape on top of the umbrella, which is then launched as a spinning projectile. Its large hitbox will trap opponents that get caught in it. Holding the button down delays its release, but it's harmless before it's fired.
Move Command
{{{keycommand}}}


Skill Card 7
物質と反物質の宇宙 (Busshitsu to Tanbusshitsu no Uchuu)
Universe of Matter and Antimatter
Cost
1 orb
Comment
単体では何の効果も無いスキマ
二つ目を配置することで引き合い
接触すると互いが反応して巨大な
歪みに変化し攻撃を行う
Translation
Places a gap that has no effect on its own. Upon placing a second, the two will be drawn together and react with each other on contact, creating a huge distortion attack.
Move Command
{{{keycommand}}}


Skill Card 8
肉体分解機 (Nikutai Bunkaiki)
Flesh Dismantler
Cost
1 orb
Comment
隙間を潜行し飛び出しつつ攻撃
ホールドで潜行状態をキープし
左右で移動が可能
Translation
Sinks into a gap, then attacks while lunging out. Holding the button allows you to stay in the gap and move left and right.
Move Command
{{{keycommand}}}


Skill Card 9
魅惑のエサ (Miwaku no Esa)
Bewitching Bait
Cost
1 orb
Comment
照準を出現させ、そこへ向け
高速で飛来する物体を発射する
照準地点は固定だが弾丸の角度は
毎回ランダムという特徴がある
Translation
Creates a crosshair through which high-speed flying objects are shot. The crosshair's position is fixed, but the projectiles' paths are random.

Spell Cards[edit]



Spell Card 1
境符「四重結界」 (Kyoufu "Shijuu Kekkai")
Border Sign "Quadruple Barrier"
Cost
3 cards
Comment
手元に複数の結界を重ねバリアを
形成し近くの敵を攻撃する
発生が早く無敵時間も有り
接近戦で切り替えしの要となる
Translation
Creates several overlapping boundaries, forming a barrier that can hit nearby opponents. It's quick and has invincibility frames, so it can turn the tables in close combat.


Spell Card 2
式神「八雲藍」 (Shikigami "Yakumo Ran")
Shikigami "Ran Yakumo"
Cost
3 cards
Comment
藍を呼び出し攻撃指示を出す
藍は直進し、敵を捕捉すると回転
敵に攻撃を加える
Translation
Summons Ran and commands her to attack. Ran charges straight ahead, and adds damage with a spinning attack if she catches the enemy.


Spell Card 3
境符「二次元と三次元の境界」 (Kyoufu "Nijigen to Sanjigen no Kyoukai")
Border Sign "Boundary of 2D and 3D"
Cost
1 card
Comment
壁に沿って境界が走る特殊な技
境界は数回方向を変え画面に残り
攻撃を加えるトラップとなる
射出時キー入力で後ろにも撃てる
Translation
A unique skill that sends a boundary running along the screen edge. It lingers on the screen as a damaging trap, changing directions several times. You can also use the arrow keys to fire it behind you.


Spell Card 4
結界「魅力的な四重結界」 (Kekkai "Miryokuteki na Shijuu Kekkai")
Barrier "Charming Quadruple Barrier"
Cost
4 cards
Comment
四重結界を巨大化し、さらに
吸引効果も付与した上級スペル
判定の巨大化に加え引き寄せる為
有効射程は格段に広くなった
Translation
A higher-level version of Quadruple Barrier that's both much larger and sucks the opponent in, markedly improving its effective range.


Spell Card 5
式神「橙」 (Shikigami "Chen")
Shikigami "Chen"
Cost
1 card
Comment
藍の式神である橙を間借りする
橙は画面内をバウンドし敵を攻撃
藍より間接的な挙動をする
式の格が低い分カード消費も低い
Translation
Borrows Ran's shikigami Chen for a while. Chen bounces across the screen, attacking the enemy with less direct motion than Ran. Being a low level shikigami, her cost is low too.


Spell Card 6
結界「客観結界」 (Kekkai "Kyakkan Kekkai")
Barrier "Objective Barrier"
Cost
3 cards
Comment
画面端全面を走る四連結界を発射
画面上の全ての端から攻撃
相手の安全な領域を削り苦しめる
Translation
Fires a set of four boundaries that traverse the edges of the screen. Since it attacks from every edge at once, it leaves little safe room for the enemy.


Spell Card 7
幻巣 「飛光虫ネスト」 (Gensou "Hikouchuu Nesuto")
Fantasy Nest "Flying Noctilucae's Nest" [1]
Cost
3 cards
Comment
紫の周囲に無数の隙間が開き
そこから前方へ弾幕を形成する
敵が背後を取ると射出方向も反転
前後に隙は無い
Translation
Opens numerous gaps around Yukari that fire danmaku forward. Even when the enemy gets behind Yukari, the firing direction will flip to follow them.


Spell Card 8
空餌 「中毒性のあるエサ」 (Kuuji "Chuudokusei no Aru Esa")
Aerial Bait "Addictive Bait"
Cost
2 cards
Comment
画面上に多数のサイトを展開
通過する高速飛行物体で攻撃する
影響の及ぶ範囲が広く、攻撃が
始まると敵は動き難い
Translation
Opens up multiple sites on the screen through which high-speed flying objects pass and attack. Since its effect covers a wide range, it'll be hard for the opponent to move once it's started.


Spell Card 9
廃線 「ぶらり廃駅下車の旅」 (Haisen "Burari Haieki Gesha no Tabi")
Abandoned Line "Aimless Journey to the Abandoned Station"
Cost
5 cards
Comment
隙間から廃電車を突撃させる大技
電車はグレイズもガードも不能で
威力も非常に高い
その代償に隙間の展開に長時間を
要する欠点がある
Translation
A dramatic move that sends an abandoned train charging forth from a gap. The train can't be grazed or guarded, and the damage is extremely high. The downside is that opening the gap takes a long time.

Notes[edit]

  1. ↑ 1.0 1.1 飛光虫 (hikouchuu): a pun on hikou (飛行, flight) and yakouchuu (夜光虫, the micro-organism Noctiluca.) Possibly a reference to one of Lafcadio Hearn's writings, entitled 'Noctilucae'. (JP, EN)
  2. ↑ Pun on 'Yakumo' and kumo, meaning 'spider'.
  3. ↑ 開けて悔しき玉手箱 (literally 'treasure box that brings regret when opened'): an idiom referring to something that seemed good but turned out badly.
  4. ↑ An idiom meaning 'to live a secluded life amidst nature'.
  5. ↑ A warped version of the above idiom, originating from a story in the Annals of Jin (晋書). In it, Sun Chu accidentally mixed up his intended words ('rest on stone'), resulting in a nonsense expression ('rinse with stone') that he quickly came up with a smart explanation for.
  6. ↑ Shortened version of the phrase "人間到る処に青山有り". Literally means 'a (final) resting place exists wherever people go'; similar to English expressions such as "home is where one lays one's head".
